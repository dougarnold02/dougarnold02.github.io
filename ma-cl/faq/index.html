    • What is the typical profile of students on the MA in CL?

    I don't think there is one, really, but there are some tendencies. We require students to have a good background in Linguistics, or Computer Science, or a related discipline (such as Psychology). Most students have more background in Linguistics than in computation -- relatively few have a strong background in programming (though there are exceptions).

    What are the job prospects for a graduate of the MA in CL?

    The demand for people with a CL background is very considerable, and the prospects are very good.

    Students who do well on the course have an excellent chance of getting a job or a research position (including a PhD place) in Computational Linguistics, providing they are prepared to travel. A glance at the Alumnus Page will show that there are graduates of the course all over Europe doing PhDs, working for `Language Engineering' companies, teaching in Universities.

    Of course, the same page will also show that there are others who go back home and do essentially what they were doing before, and many who drop out of touch quite quickly.

    You can get an idea of the sorts of jobs that are available to someone with a background in Computational Linguistics from several sites, for example:

    • http://www.elsnet.org/jobs/
